## FAQ Section

### What is post-quantum cryptography?
Post-quantum cryptography (PQC) refers to cryptographic algorithms designed to be secure against attacks from quantum computers. AMCIS implements NIST-approved PQC standards including CRYSTALS-Kyber for key encapsulation and CRYSTALS-Dilithium for digital signatures.

### How does AMCIS automate federal compliance?
AMCIS continuously monitors your security controls, maps them to federal frameworks (NIST CSF, FedRAMP, CMMC), generates compliance reports, and provides real-time dashboards showing your compliance posture.

### Can AMCIS work in air-gapped environments?
Yes, AMCIS supports fully air-gapped deployments for classified and isolated networks. All threat intelligence, updates, and AI models can be transferred via secure physical media.

### What makes AMCIS different from other security platforms?
AMCIS is the first unified platform combining post-quantum cryptography, AI governance, and federal compliance automation. Unlike point solutions, AMCIS provides comprehensive coverage across all security domains.
